About the Initiative

Sita Anandam Cancer Care Initiative

A collaborative effort to bring early cancer detection, awareness, and treatment support to the most marginalised — because no one should lose their life to a disease that can be treated, simply because they couldn't access or afford care.

The Challenge

Cancer is treatable. But for millions, the belief that it isn't becomes a death sentence.

Among the most marginalised communities, cancer carries a fatalism that prevents people from seeking help. The word itself is enough to make families give up hope. Myths, stigma, and fear combine with a lack of access to create a devastating gap between what medicine can do and what reaches those who need it most.

The idea is simple but transformative: identify cancer as early as possible, break the myths that prevent people from seeking care, and financially support patients through treatment — so that a diagnosis does not become a verdict.

How We Support

Financial & Facilitation Support

We provide direct financial support covering everything from initial screening to treatment — until patients can access government schemes or NGO support. We also facilitate that transition.

  • Travel costs for hospital visits and diagnostic tests
  • Initial consultations and follow-up screenings
  • Treatment costs, medication, and hospital stays
  • Linkage to Ayushman Bharat, PMJAY, and NGO schemes
  • Ongoing coordination between all partners
A Collective Effort

Four Partners, One Mission

This initiative works because each partner brings something essential.

Funding & Medical Oversight

Dr. M.S. Vidyasagar

Retired Head of Department, Radiation Oncology — Kasturba Medical College, Manipal, Karnataka

Funds the entire initiative and provides remote medical oversight — reviewing patient profiles, tracking updates, and offering clinical guidance.

Field Operations & Awareness

MNREGA Watch

Grassroots organisation present across all blocks of Muzaffarpur

Organises health camps in panchayats, spreads cancer awareness, and identifies suspected cases — reaching people who would never walk into a hospital on their own.

Clinical Care & Treatment

Dr. Ravikant Singh & Team

Officer-in-Charge, Homi Bhabha Cancer Hospital & Research Centre, Muzaffarpur

Participates in field camps for screening and provides the clinical backbone — diagnosis, treatment, and ongoing medical care for confirmed patients.

Coordination & Facilitation

GRAMA

Coordination and patient tracking

Tracks every patient's journey from screening to treatment, bridges gaps between partners, facilitates hospital visits, manages records, and connects patients to government and NGO support.
